The arithmetic mean, commonly referred to as the "mean" or "average," is a measure of central tendency that is calculated by summing a set of values and then dividing by the number of values. It provides a simple way to summarize a collection of numbers with a single value.

The formula for calculating the arithmetic mean is given by:

Mean (x) = (x₁ + x₂ + x₃ + ... + xₙ) / n

Where:

  • x₁, x₂, x₃, ..., xₙ are the individual values in the dataset.
  • n is the total number of values.

For example, consider the following set of numbers: 4, 8, 6, 5, and 3. To calculate the arithmetic mean:

1. First, sum the values: 4 + 8 + 6 + 5 + 3 = 26.

2. Next, divide the sum by the number of values: 26 / 5 = 5.2.

Therefore, the arithmetic mean of the set is 5.2.
